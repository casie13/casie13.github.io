How remote-proctoring surveillance reshapes pedagogy

A graduate-level multi-year end-to-end qualitative research project examining privacy perceptions of emerging e-proctoring software popularized in higher education during Covid-19.

roleLead researcher
methodsQual thematic analysis
contextCHORUS Lab, Carleton
outputsSOUPS '22 · EuroUSEC '23 · TOCHI (in review)
tl;dr
summaryCovid-19 introduced e-proctoring software into universities. Students protested, instructors grappled. I did a multi-study project investigating students' and instructors' privacy perceptions, and provide actionable recommendations for instructors, universities, and edTech software designers.
